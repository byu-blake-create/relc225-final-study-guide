Unit 1

Understanding Latter-day Saint Doctrine and History

1.1What are the six foundational time periods of Church history covered in this class (titles and years)?

New York/Pennsylvania, 1805-1830; Ohio/Missouri, 1831-1838; Nauvoo, 1839-1846; Pioneer/Utah, 1846-1898; expansion/adjustment, 1898-1951; worldwide Church, 1951-present.

1.2What is “doctrine”?

Doctrine is revealed, eternal gospel truth about God, His plan, and salvation through Jesus Christ.

1.3What is the difference in doctrine, principles, covenants, policies, programs, etc.?

Doctrine is eternal truth; principles guide action; ordinances are sacred priesthood acts; covenants are promises with God; policies, programs, and practices are changeable applications.

1.4What are four levels of evaluating an official Church teaching?

Evaluate by canonized scripture/official declarations, united First Presidency and Twelve teachings, repeated official teachings, and individual leader statements.

1.5What are five factors for evaluating a historical source?

Check whether a source is primary, close to the event, authored by a reliable witness, shaped by bias or purpose, and corroborated by other evidence.

Unit 2

The First Vision

2.1What is the Great Apostasy?

The Great Apostasy is the loss of the fulness of Christ’s Church, including priesthood authority and ordinances, after the deaths of the ancient Apostles.

2.2How many primary accounts of the First Vision did Joseph write; when did he write them?

Four primary accounts: 1832, 1835, 1838, and 1842. The 1838 account became Joseph Smith-History.

2.3What was the audience and context for each of Joseph’s four primary First Vision accounts?

1832: personal history and forgiveness. 1835: told to Robert Matthews. 1838: official Church history. 1842: public summary in the Wentworth Letter.

2.4What are two primary doctrinal messages from the First Vision?

God the Father and Jesus Christ are distinct living beings, and the Restoration was necessary because the fulness of authority and ordinances had been lost.

Unit 3

The Book of Mormon

3.1How many years passed from Moroni’s first visit to Joseph Smith until Joseph could obtain the gold plates?

Four years: Moroni first appeared in September 1823, and Joseph received the plates in September 1827.

3.2What is the definition of “Urim and Thummim”?

The Urim and Thummim means divinely prepared instruments for revelation or translation; in Book of Mormon history it usually refers to the Nephite interpreters, sometimes broadly to seer stones.

3.3How was the Book of Mormon translated?

Joseph translated by “the gift and power of God,” dictating English text while using the interpreters or a seer stone.

3.4Who were the Three Witnesses of the Book of Mormon plates?

Oliver Cowdery, David Whitmer, and Martin Harris.

Unit 4

Restoration of the Priesthood and the Founding of the Church

4.1What priesthood and keys did John the Baptist restore?

John the Baptist restored the Aaronic Priesthood, including keys of repentance, baptism, and the ministering of angels.

4.2What priesthood and keys did Peter, James, and John restore?

Peter, James, and John restored the Melchizedek Priesthood and apostolic authority, including authority to confer the Holy Ghost and preside.

4.3What occurred in the “chamber of father Whitmer,” and what is its relationship to the office of Elder?

In the Whitmer home, Joseph and Oliver exercised authority tied to organizing the Church and ordaining elders, an office central to preaching, confirming, and presiding.

4.4How, when, and where was the Church organized?

The Church was organized April 6, 1830, at the Peter Whitmer Sr. home in Fayette, New York, with Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery sustained as presiding elders.

Unit 5

History and Purpose of the Doctrine and Covenants

5.1What are the Manuscript Revelation Books?

The Manuscript Revelation Books are early handwritten collections of Joseph Smith’s revelations, preserving texts later published in the Doctrine and Covenants.

5.2What is the 1833 Book of Commandments?

The 1833 Book of Commandments was the first attempted printed collection of revelations; printing was stopped when a mob destroyed the Independence press.

5.3What were the original “doctrine” and “covenants” parts that created the book’s title?

In 1835, “doctrine” referred mainly to the Lectures on Faith, and “covenants” referred to Joseph Smith’s revelations.

5.4What are several key principles to remember when considering Doctrine and Covenants revelations—including why written revelations have sometimes been altered?

D&C revelations came in historical settings and were sometimes edited, clarified, or expanded for publication as circumstances and understanding developed.

5.5What are the Stewardship Principle, the Brethren Principle, and the Front Door Principle pertaining to personal revelation?

Stewardship: revelation comes within responsibility. Brethren: Churchwide revelation comes through presiding leaders. Front Door: God works through orderly, recognized channels.

Unit 6

Additional Restoration Scripture

6.1What does it mean that Joseph Smith “translated” the Bible?

Joseph’s Bible “translation” was an inspired revision, not a normal language translation from Hebrew or Greek manuscripts.

6.2By what names have Joseph's Bible translation been known?

New Translation, Inspired Version, and Joseph Smith Translation (JST).

6.3What types of changes to the Bible did Joseph Smith make?

He made clarifications, doctrinal expansions, wording corrections, harmonizations, restored material, and inspired additions.

6.4Where did the Book of Abraham come from?

The Book of Abraham came through Joseph Smith’s work with Egyptian papyri acquired in 1835; faithful views include direct-translation and catalyst/revelatory models.

6.5How did we get The Pearl of Great Price, and when was it canonized?

Franklin D. Richards compiled the Pearl of Great Price in 1851; the Church canonized it in 1880.

Unit 7

Zion, Gathering of Israel, and the Law of Consecration

7.1Where was the Church first commanded to gather in this dispensation?

The Saints were first commanded to gather to Ohio in 1831; Jackson County, Missouri, was later identified as Zion’s center place.

7.2What is the city of Zion, or the New Jerusalem?

Zion/New Jerusalem is a holy covenant community and latter-day city built by the pure in heart under God’s law.

7.3Where will the latter-day city of Zion be built?

The center place is Independence, Jackson County, Missouri, though “Zion” also refers broadly to the pure in heart and stakes worldwide.

7.4What was Joseph Smith’s vision for Zion?

Joseph envisioned a temple-centered, consecrated, spiritually united society prepared to receive Christ.

7.5What Doctrine and Covenants section is known as “The Law” of the Church?

Doctrine and Covenants 42.

7.6What are the definitions and differences of “all things common”, the United Order (Firm), and the law of consecration?

“All things common” is economic unity; the United Firm was a specific 1830s stewardship organization; consecration is the enduring covenant to use all we have for God.

Unit 8

Kirtland Revelation

8.1Which Doctrine and Covenants section is the great revelation on the three degrees of heavenly glory?

Doctrine and Covenants 76, “The Vision,” received by Joseph Smith and Sidney Rigdon in 1832.

8.2What was Joseph Smith doing immediately prior to receiving Doctrine and Covenants 76?

Joseph and Sidney were working on the inspired translation of John 5:29 about resurrection and judgment.

8.3What is a central message of Doctrine and Covenants 76?

Christ’s Atonement is expansive: nearly all inherit glory, while celestial exaltation requires receiving Christ, ordinances, covenants, and overcoming by faith.

8.4“The Vision” actually consists of how many smaller individual visions? What are they?

Commonly six scenes: the Father and Son, Lucifer’s fall, sons of perdition, celestial glory, terrestrial glory, and telestial glory.

8.5What are the general descriptions of who enters outer darkness, the telestial, terrestrial, and celestial degrees of heaven?

Outer darkness is for sons of perdition. Telestial is for the unrepentant wicked eventually redeemed; terrestrial for honorable but not fully valiant; celestial for covenant disciples who receive Christ and overcome.

8.6Which Doctrine and Covenants section teaches that Christ continued from grace to grace, until He received a fulness?

Doctrine and Covenants 93.

8.7How can we each also receive of a fulness?

We receive grace for grace by coming to Christ, keeping commandments, receiving truth and light, and remaining faithful to covenants.

8.8What is the eternal nature of the mind/spirit?

D&C 93 teaches intelligence, or the light of truth, is eternal and was not created from nothing.

8.9What was the purpose of the original School of the Prophets?

It prepared elders for ministry, missions, spiritual power, learning, unity, and the promised Kirtland endowment.

8.10What practices were implemented in the School of the Prophets to help accomplish its objectives?

Participants studied doctrine and secular subjects, prayed, worshipped, used sacred greetings, practiced washing of feet, and sought spiritual gifts.

8.11What were the circumstances that preceded Joseph Smith receiving the Word of Wisdom?

The revelation came in 1833 during the School of the Prophets, partly after concerns about tobacco use and cleanliness in the meeting room.

8.12How and when did the Word of Wisdom become a binding commandment?

It developed gradually: first “not by commandment,” then urged by Brigham Young in 1851, and made a firm temple standard in the early 20th century.

8.13What is a doctrinal definition of endowment?

An endowment is a divine gift of power, knowledge, covenants, and priesthood-connected blessings that prepares disciples to return to God.

8.14What was the Kirtland “endowment,” and how did it develop?

The Kirtland endowment was an endowment of power tied to washings, anointings, solemn assemblies, spiritual gifts, and the 1836 temple dedication.

8.15Who are the heavenly beings who restored priesthood keys, as recorded in Doctrine and Covenants 110?

Christ appeared first; then Moses restored gathering keys, Elias committed the gospel of Abraham, and Elijah restored sealing keys.

Unit 9

Kirtland Opposition and Missouri Expulsion

9.1What were the primary reasons that the Saints experienced problems in Zion?

Problems came from outside fears of Mormon immigration, politics, religion, and anti-slavery perceptions, plus internal pride, contention, and lack of consecration.

9.2According to the Lord, why did the city of Zion fail to be established?

D&C 101 and 105 say Zion was not established because the Saints were not sufficiently united, obedient, humble, and consecrated.

9.3What was the Kirtland Safety Society?

It was a Kirtland banking venture that failed during the Panic of 1837, causing losses and dissent.

9.4What are some of the reasons for the apostasy that occurred in Kirtland?

Financial collapse, failed expectations, criticism of Joseph Smith, pride, jealousy, speculation, and personal conflict all fueled apostasy.

9.5Where was the Church headquartered after the Saints left Independence, Missouri and Kirtland, Ohio?

Far West, Missouri, became headquarters in 1838; after Missouri, the Saints gathered to Commerce/Nauvoo, Illinois.

9.6Where is Adam-ondi-Ahman, and why is it important?

Adam-ondi-Ahman is in Daviess County, Missouri; D&C 116 links it to Adam and a future sacred meeting.

9.7What were the “Salt Sermon” and the “July 4th Oration,” and who gave them?

Sidney Rigdon gave both 1838 speeches; they denounced dissenters and warned enemies, intensifying fears.

9.8What was the Missouri “Mormon War” of 1838?

It was the 1838 armed conflict in Missouri involving Saints, mobs, militias, raids, Hawn’s Mill, the Extermination Order, imprisonments, and expulsion.

9.9Who were the “Danites”?

The Danites were a secret militant group in Far West, associated with Sampson Avard, formed amid fears and conflict in 1838.

9.10What happened at Gallatin, Missouri?

A voting-day brawl at Gallatin in August 1838 and later Latter-day Saint raids helped escalate the Missouri conflict.

9.11What happened at Hawn’s Mill?

On October 30, 1838, a Missouri militia mob killed 17 Saints at Hawn’s Mill.

9.12What is the Missouri Extermination Order, and who signed it?

Governor Lilburn W. Boggs’s 1838 order directed that the Saints be driven from Missouri or “exterminated.” It was rescinded in 1976.

9.13What were the circumstances surrounding Joseph Smith and other Church leaders being incarcerated in 1838–1839?

After Far West surrendered, Joseph Smith and others were arrested on charges including treason and held in Liberty Jail through winter 1838-1839.

9.14At the time it was written, to whom did Doctrine and Covenants 121:13–23, 39 refer?

Initially it referred to Missouri persecutors and apostates harming the Saints; verse 39 also states the broader danger of unrighteous dominion.

9.15Where did the Saints flee when they were expelled from Missouri?

They fled to Illinois, especially Quincy, then gathered to Commerce/Nauvoo.

Unit 10

Nauvoo Period

10.1How was the city of Nauvoo established as the headquarters of the Church?

The Saints bought land at Commerce, Illinois, in 1839, renamed it Nauvoo, secured a charter, and built it as their new gathering place.

10.2Who was called as the Church Assistant President (Doctrine and Covenants 124)?

Hyrum Smith.

10.3Who served in the First Presidency in Doctrine and Covenants 124?

Joseph Smith, Sidney Rigdon, and Hyrum Smith; William Law also soon served in Nauvoo-era First Presidency leadership.

10.4What was the Nauvoo House?

A commanded Nauvoo boardinghouse/hotel for visitors and a residence connected to Joseph Smith’s family.

10.5What historical events preceded Joseph Smith’s announcement of baptism for the dead in Nauvoo?

Background included Alvin Smith’s death, D&C 137, questions about the unevangelized dead, and Seymour Brunson’s 1840 funeral sermon.

10.6What are the primary doctrinal teachings relating to vicarious work for the dead?

The living may perform saving ordinances for the dead by priesthood authority; the dead retain agency to accept or reject them.

10.7What is the modern offering of the sons of Levi?

The modern “offering in righteousness” is often understood as worthy temple and family-history work, including records and ordinances for the dead.

10.8What historical factors led to the creation of the Nauvoo Relief Society?

Sarah Kimball and Margaret Cook organized sewing help for temple workers; Joseph expanded it into the Female Relief Society of Nauvoo in 1842.

10.9What are the stated purposes of the Relief Society?

Relieve the poor, save souls, strengthen virtue, support temple work, and organize women for gospel service.

10.10What do the experiences of women in Nauvoo teach us about the relationship of women, the temple, and the priesthood?

Nauvoo women received temple ordinances and priesthood-linked covenant power, though they were not ordained to priesthood offices like men.

10.11What was the “Voice of Innocence” document?

It was a 1844 Relief Society statement defending Nauvoo women against immoral accusations; the setting was complicated by secret plural marriage.

10.12When was the Relief Society disbanded? Why did it occur?

It stopped meeting after March 1844, largely because of plural marriage tensions, leadership concerns, the martyrdom, and the coming exodus.

10.13How and where was the temple endowment introduced in Nauvoo?

Joseph introduced it May 4, 1842, in the upper room of the Red Brick Store, then prepared men and women to administer it in the Nauvoo Temple.

10.14What are the keys to detecting true messengers from false ones?

D&C 129 gives tests for messengers; broadly, true messengers are discerned by revelation, priesthood keys, and fidelity to Christ.

10.15What are the apostasy and adoption theories regarding the relationship between Freemasonry and the source of the temple endowment ceremony?

Masonry likely influenced some outward forms; Latter-day Saints understand the endowment’s authority, covenants, and saving meaning as revealed.

10.16What is the central premise of the King Follett Discourse?

God is an exalted personal being, humans are eternal in nature, and through Christ they can become like God; the sermon is influential but not canonized.

Unit 11

Eternal Marriage and Plural Marriage

11.1What is the definition of the “new and everlasting covenant of marriage”?

It is eternal marriage sealed by priesthood authority and ratified by the Holy Spirit of Promise.

11.2What ordinances are required for exaltation and to become like God?

Exaltation is tied to baptism, confirmation, priesthood ordination for men, temple initiatory and endowment, eternal marriage sealing, and enduring faith in Christ.

11.3What is meant by “eternal increase”?

Eternal increase means continuing family, posterity, creative power, and divine life in exaltation.

11.4What is meant by “the more sure word of prophecy” or “calling and election sure”?

It is a divine assurance of eternal life, sealed by the Holy Spirit of Promise, conditional on continued faithfulness.

11.5What are the Church’s teachings related to those people who don’t have the opportunity to marry in this life?

No blessing will be denied to faithful people because of circumstances beyond their control; God will provide just opportunities.

11.6What is the context and content of Doctrine and Covenants 132?

D&C 132, recorded in 1843, teaches sealing power, eternal marriage, exaltation, the Holy Spirit of Promise, and plural marriage for that commanded period.

11.7What are the details of Joseph Smith’s practice of plural marriage (when it began, how implemented, how many wives, ages, relationships with them, etc.)?

Joseph began teaching/practicing plural marriage by the 1830s-early 1840s and was sealed to about 30-40 women. Ages and circumstances varied, including some teenage and polyandrous sealings; the record is incomplete and relationship details differ by case.

11.8What is polyandry? What is polygyny?

Polyandry is one woman married/sealed to more than one man; polygyny is one man married/sealed to more than one woman.

11.9According to Doctrine and Covenants 132, what are three reasons why the Church practiced plural marriage during the 19th century?

Reasons include restoring ancient things, raising covenant posterity when commanded, and proving/binding Saints through Abrahamic sacrifice and sealing.

11.10What are some of the theological concepts associated with plural marriage, including dynastic sealings?

It involved sealing power, Abrahamic covenant, eternal increase, sacrifice, and dynastic sealings that linked families to priesthood leaders before later ancestor-focused practice.

11.11What is the Church’s standing law of marriage?

Monogamy is God’s standing law unless He commands otherwise; new plural marriages are not authorized today.

Unit 12

Martyrdom, Succession and Western Exodus

12.1Why was Joseph Smith killed? (List reasons from John Taylor’s 1854 talk.)

John Taylor taught Joseph died for his prophetic witness, the Book of Mormon, restored priesthood and ordinances, gathering Israel, temple work, and testimony of Christ.

12.2Who were the Laws, the Fosters, and the Higbees, and how were they connected to the martyrdom?

The Laws, Fosters, and Higbees were Nauvoo dissenters who opposed Joseph and helped support the Nauvoo Expositor crisis.

12.3What was the Nauvoo Expositor?

A dissident newspaper published June 7, 1844, criticizing Joseph’s power, plural marriage, and doctrine; its destruction led to legal crisis.

12.4Who was Thomas Ford, and what was his role related to the martyrdom?

Thomas Ford was Illinois governor; he promised protection but failed to prevent the Carthage mob attack.

12.5On what legal charges was Joseph Smith incarcerated in Carthage Jail?

Riot for the Expositor destruction, then treason against Illinois after martial law was declared in Nauvoo.

12.6What was the succession crisis of 1844, and who were the key players?

After Joseph and Hyrum died, claimants included Sidney Rigdon, Brigham Young/the Twelve, James Strang, William Marks, Lyman Wight, William Smith, and later Joseph Smith III.

12.7What were the supposed successors’ various claims to authority?

Rigdon claimed guardianship; the Twelve claimed transferred keys; Strang claimed appointment; Marks claimed stake authority; Smith-family claimants used lineal succession; Wight pursued western plans.

12.8What is the Church’s process of prophetic succession today?

The First Presidency dissolves, the Twelve preside, and the senior apostle is sustained and set apart as the next President.

12.9What are the primary Joseph Smith-centric restoration churches, and how were they established?

Major groups include LDS under Brigham Young, Community of Christ under Joseph Smith III, Strangites, Church of Christ (Temple Lot), and Bickertonite/Rigdonite-linked churches.

12.10What was the Council of Fifty?

A 1844 council exploring religious liberty, political kingdom ideas, Joseph’s presidential campaign, and possible western settlement.

12.11What factors contributed to the Saints’ western exodus and settlement in Utah?

Violence, repeal of the Nauvoo charter, anti-Mormon pressure, desire for temple ordinances, and need for refuge pushed the Saints west.

Unit 13

Official Declarations and Modern Revelation

13.1When and where did the Church begin to do vicarious endowments?

In the St. George Utah Temple in 1877.

13.2Second only to Brigham Young, who was the person largely in charge of making temple worship central to the Saints?

Wilford Woodruff.

13.3In conjunction with sealings, what is the law of adoption? How and when did it end?

It sealed adults to priesthood “fathers” or leaders; Wilford Woodruff ended it in 1894 by directing Saints to seal to their own ancestors.

13.4What is the name of the federal law that disenfranchised the Church and seized its assets?

The Edmunds-Tucker Act of 1887.

13.5How, when, and why was Official Declaration 1 released?

Wilford Woodruff issued the Manifesto in September 1890; the Church accepted it October 6, 1890, to end new plural marriages and preserve temples and ordinances amid federal pressure.

13.6What is the Second Manifesto, and who issued it?

Joseph F. Smith’s 1904 statement enforcing the end of new plural marriages worldwide, with discipline for violators.

13.7What is the historicity of restricting members of African descent from priesthood offices (when, who, and why it was started)?

No universal restriction is known under Joseph Smith; it developed under Brigham Young in the late 1840s-1850s. The Church says no clear revealed origin is known.

13.8What is the Church’s doctrine regarding race?

All are God’s children and may receive priesthood and temple blessings if worthy; the Church condemns racism and rejects past racial theories.

13.9What were the names of the black men who were ordained to the priesthood during Joseph Smith’s time?

Elijah Abel and Q. Walker Lewis are the best documented; Joseph T. Ball is also commonly included.

13.10When and by whom did the priesthood restriction begin?

It emerged under Brigham Young, especially 1847-1852; no canonized revelation instituting it is known.

13.11What were some of the factors that contributed to the restriction on members of African descent receiving the priesthood?

Likely factors include 19th-century racism, slavery debates, anti-interracial-marriage fears, biblical curse theories, and territorial politics; these are not doctrinal justifications.

13.12What is Official Declaration 2, and who received the revelation on that subject?

Official Declaration 2 announced the 1978 revelation extending priesthood and temple blessings to all worthy members; President Spencer W. Kimball received it in the Salt Lake Temple.

13.13What are the Church’s official teachings today regarding why members of African descent were denied priesthood offices and temple ordinances?

The Church does not claim to know the precise reason and disavows past explanations based on curses, premortal conduct, racial inferiority, or opposition to interracial marriage.

13.14Who received Doctrine and Covenants 138, and what were the circumstances surrounding its receipt?

Joseph F. Smith received D&C 138 on October 3, 1918, while pondering 1 Peter and grieving amid war, influenza, and family deaths.

13.15What is the difference between scripture and canon?

Scripture is inspired word from God; canon is the official body of scripture accepted as binding standard works.

13.16What new doctrines are taught in Doctrine and Covenants 138?

Christ organized righteous spirits to preach in the spirit world; missionary work and redemption of the dead continue beyond mortality.

13.17What is the doctrinal status of The Proclamation on the Family?

It is an official prophetic proclamation from the First Presidency and Twelve, authoritative but not canonized in the standard works.

13.18What are the doctrines within The Proclamation on the Family regarding: (a) marriage and procreation, (b) the duties of parents for happy families, (c) the responsibilities of fathers and mothers, and (d) warnings and admonitions to society?

Marriage between man and woman is ordained of God; procreation belongs within marriage; parents should teach and nurture children; fathers and mothers are equal partners with distinct responsibilities; family breakdown and abuse bring accountability.

Unit 14

The Restitution of All Things

14.1Why is continuing revelation necessary?

Because Christ leads a living Church, circumstances change, and the Restoration unfolds line upon line.

14.2How is doctrine established for the Church?

Doctrine is established by revelation through authorized prophets and apostles, especially united First Presidency and Twelve teaching, scripture, and official Church action.

14.3What is a difference between prophets leading the Church astray and making personal mistakes?

The Lord will not let the President lead the Church into binding apostasy, but prophets can still have mortal limits, opinions, and mistakes.

14.4According to Doctrine and Covenants 21, what two words define how we are to follow living prophets?

“Patience” and “faith” (D&C 21:5).

14.5When will the Restoration be complete?

The Restoration continues until Christ restores all things, gathers Israel, establishes Zion, completes His work, and returns.

14.6How can we participate in the ongoing restoration?

By keeping covenants, doing temple and family-history work, gathering Israel, building Zion, serving others, and following living prophets.
